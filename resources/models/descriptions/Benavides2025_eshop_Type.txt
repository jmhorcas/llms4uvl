A fictitious eShop product line. 
eShop has two mandatory and three optional child features.
Any configuration of an eShop has to have a Catalogue and a Security option.
A configuration of an eShop can optionally have SEO, Payment, and Platform support. 
Exactly one Security option (Standard or High) and exactly one Payment option can be selected. 
The Payment of the eShop must be either Bank Transfer or Credit card (but not both in the same configuration).
Whenever Platform is selected, the customer has three Platform options to choose from: Mobile App, Browser, Desktop App.
The customer needs to select between two and three platform features.
An eShop including Credit card must include High security support.
The Bank Transfer feature cannot be combined with a Mobile App, i.e., these two features are incompatible.
In addition there are some attributes that provide information on the respective features.
We have a number attribute (price), a Boolean attribute (SEPA), and a string attribute (URL):
High 100, Standard 50, Bank Transfer 10 and SEPA, SEO 40, Credit Card 20, Desktop App 70, Mobile App 80, and Browser 20.
The overall sum in price of all selected features should be smaller than 200.
The user can decide to have between one and five Catalogue features. 
A customer may select varying catalogues for different markets, e.g., Europe and North America. 
Note that each selected Catalogue would increase the overall price by 30.
The customer can now configure an integer feature Items in Basket, which can be used to limit the maximum number of
items a customer can put in his basket at the same time. 
A cross-tree constraint ensures that the maximum number of items is higher than zero. 
Further, Browser is now a string feature where the URL can be directly configured. 
Another cross-tree constraint denotes that the URL may not be longer than 30 characters.
